## Approach

rs-tfhe-style **N=1024** parameters (the [rs-tfhe](https://github.com/thedonutfactory/rs-tfhe)
128-bit gate-bootstrap shape), adapted to this challenge's q=2^64 / 4-message space. Halving
the ring degree from the N=2048 baseline roughly halves the FFT work. Same f64-FFT CGGI
bootstrap; **only `src/algorithm/` changed** (parameters + decomposition).

- LWE: `n=768`, `σ=2^46` → **132-bit**. (rs-tfhe uses n=700; our 4-message space has a
  tighter noise budget than its boolean gate, so a slightly larger n lets `lwe_sigma` drop
  and cuts key-switch noise.)
- GLWE: `k=1, N=1024`, `σ=2^39` → **129.6-bit**.
- `pbs ℓ=3 baselog=7` (21-bit), `ks ℓ=8 baselog=2` (fine key-switch). Output noise σ≈2^55.3
  ⇒ failure margin ~4.7 bits (≈ 2^-130).

The SPQLIOS AVX/FMA assembly FFT that gives rs-tfhe its ~15 ms is a C++/assembly build
(`cc` + `build.rs`) outside `src/algorithm/`, so it cannot be ported here; with the
generic rustfft this lands ~32 ms. A hand-written `core::arch` AVX FFT inside `src/algorithm/`
(allowed) is the path toward rs-tfhe's speed.
